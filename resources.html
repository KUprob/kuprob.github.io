  • (1) link Probability distribution of the free energy of the continuum directed random polymer in 1 + 1 dimensions
    We consider the continuum directed random polymer in \(1+1\) dimensions with space-time white noise potential, and compute an exact Laplace transform formula for the probability distribution of its free energy via a Fredholm determinant. This is accomplished through a rigorous version of the replica method using stochastic calculus and Feynman-Kac formulas connecting the stochastic heat equation to the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) equation. The result proves a conjecture of Kardar from 1987 for the exponent \(\beta=1/3\) in the intermediate disorder regime.
  • (2) link The KPZ fixed point
    The KPZ fixed point is a Markov process \(\mathcal{H}(t,x)\) in \(t \geq 0\), \(x \in \mathbb{R}\) conjectured to be the universal scaling limit of all models in the KPZ universality class. We introduce a Poisson point process construction of the directed landscape, and use it to construct the KPZ fixed point as a scaling limit of TASEP (totally asymmetric simple exclusion process). We show that it is a universal attractor for the KPZ class, with explicit formulas for its transition probabilities via Fredholm determinants.
  • (3) link The directed landscape
    The directed landscape is a random continuous function \(L: \mathbb{R}^4 \to \mathbb{R}\), which arises as the scaling limit of last passage percolation models. We prove its existence and basic properties as a universal limit object, showing it governs the large-scale behavior of directed random growth processes. It is constructed via a determinantal point process and satisfies a metric-like property \(L(x,s;y,t) + L(y,t;z,u) \leq L(x,s;z,u)\) for \(s \leq t \leq u\).
  • (4) link The stationary horizon and semi-infinite geodesics in the directed landscape
    We study the stationary horizon, a stationary process in the directed landscape, and semi-infinite geodesics emanating from any point \((x,t)\) in direction \(\theta \in \mathbb{R}\). We prove their almost sure coalescence and describe their Busemann functions, showing that the stationary horizon acts as an invariant measure coupling geodesic energies. The results connect KPZ universality to classical stationary percolation theory.
  • (5) link Stationary measures for integrable polymers on a strip
    We construct stationary measures for \(O(n)\)-integrable polymer models on a spatial strip \(\mathbb{Z} \times \{1,\ldots,N\}\), where weights are given by inverse-gamma distributions. For discrete and continuum limits, we show these measures are unique and explicitly computable via Bethe ansatz, providing a framework for studying KPZ-class models with boundary effects. The partition functions satisfy stochastic integrability conditions.
  • (6) link Strong Characterization for the Airy Line Ensemble
    The Airy line ensemble \(\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{A}_1, \mathcal{A}_2, \ldots)\) is a countable collection of random continuous curves appearing as a universal limit in KPZ-class models. We provide a strong characterization via a Gibbs property and Brownian bridge comparisons, proving that any ensemble satisfying these properties must equal \(\mathcal{A}\) in distribution. The top line \(\mathcal{A}_1\) relates to the Airy\(_2\) process minus a parabola.
