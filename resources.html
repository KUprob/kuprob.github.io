  • (1) link Probability distribution of the free energy of the continuum directed random polymer in 1 + 1 dimensions
    We consider the directed random polymer in 1+1 dimensions, a continuum object formally defined via a random partition function \( Z(x,t) = \mathbb{E} \left\lvert e^{\int_0^t W(B_s,s) ds} \right\rvert \) where \(W\) is a space-time white noise and the expectation is over Brownian motion paths from \((0,0)\) to \((x,t)\). Through exact computations involving chaos expansions and Fredholm determinants we find a formula for the probability distribution of the free energy \(\log Z(x,t)\), which is anticipated to be universal across a wide class of models in the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) universality class. (arXiv:1003.0443)
  • (2) link The KPZ fixed point
    An explicit Fredholm determinant formula is derived for the multipoint distribution of the height function of the totally asymmetric simple exclusion process (TASEP) with arbitrary initial condition. Through an analysis of this formula in the KPZ 1:2:3 scaling limit we prove convergence of the TASEP height function to a universal Markov process with initial data, the KPZ fixed point, whose one-point distributions are the GUE Tracy-Widom distribution or generalizations thereof depending on the initial data. (arXiv:1701.00018)
  • (3) link The directed landscape
    We study the directed landscape, a random continuous function \(L: \mathbb{R}^4 \to \mathbb{R}\) which is expected to be the scaling limit of last passage percolation models and is conjectured to be the universal scaling limit of all models in the KPZ universality class. Using purely probabilistic arguments we prove some fundamental properties of \(L\), including a connection to the Airy sheet and the non-existence of bi-infinite geodesics. (arXiv:1812.00309)
  • (4) link The stationary horizon and semi-infinite geodesics in the directed landscape
    The directed landscape is a random continuous function from \(\mathbb{R}^4\) to \(\mathbb{R}\) that arises as a universal scaling limit for models in the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) universality class. We study semi-infinite geodesics in the directed landscape, which are geodesic rays parameterized by \(\mathbb{R}\) that emanate from every space-time point in every direction \(\theta \in \mathbb{R}\). Specifically, we establish existence, uniqueness up to sets of Hausdorff dimension zero, and coalescence of these semi-infinite geodesics as well as formulas for the stationary horizon—a process that arises as a limit of Busemann functions—which altogether yield a complete characterization of the geodesic structure of the directed landscape. (arXiv:2203.13242)
  • (5) link Stationary measures for integrable polymers on a strip
    We consider discrete integrable polymer models on a strip \(\mathbb{Z}\times \{1,\dots,N\}\), where the weights are given by inverse-gamma random variables. We construct stationary measures for these models via boundary conditions involving Askey-Wilson signed measures and show that these measures converge under suitable scaling to stationary measures for the \(O(n)\) polymer on a strip for \(n=-1,0,1\). We furthermore identify these measures explicitly via their moments thus providing a new boundary characterization of the stochastic six-vertex model and the ASEP in an interval. (arXiv:2306.05983)
  • (6) link Strong Characterization for the Airy Line Ensemble
    The Airy line ensemble \(\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{A}_1, \mathcal{A}_2, \ldots)\) is a collection of random continuous functions from \(\mathbb{R}\) to \(\mathbb{R}\), which appears as a universal scaling limit of stochastic growth models and random matrix ensembles in the KPZ universality class. In this note, we provide a strong characterization of this ensemble via two properties: the Brownian Gibbs property and a characterization of the top line \(\mathcal{A}_1\) via the Airy\(_2\) process. We prove that any line ensemble satisfying these two properties must be equal in distribution to \(\mathcal{A}\).
